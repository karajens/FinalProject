Discover the Wonders of Lake Powell

Lake Powell is a large reservoir on the Colorado River, located between northern Arizona and southern Utah. Formed by Glen Canyon Dam, it stretches about 186 miles and has numerous side canyons and over 2,000 miles of shoreline. Its main water source is the Colorado River, supplemented by the San Juan, Escalante, and Dirty Devil rivers. The reservoir’s outflow passes through Glen Canyon Dam, continuing down the Colorado River into the Grand Canyon.

The Great Debate

The debate about whether to drain or keep Lake Powell boils down to questions about the environment, cultural history, and how we manage our water. Those in favor of draining believe it would bring back the lost beauty of Glen Canyon, help restore habitats, and improve water quality downstream, especially as climate change makes the lake less reliable. They also argue that the reservoir’s sediment buildup and ongoing drought conditions are signs it’s not sustainable. Meanwhile, supporters of keeping Lake Powell say it provides an important water “bank” for millions of people, supports farming, generates clean electricity, and fuels tourism. In other words, deciding what to do with Lake Powell isn’t just about a reservoir—it’s about balancing nature, people’s needs, and the future of water in a changing world.
